Design Process and Artifacts

Conceptual Design

Ambrosia was designed with a vision to bring out a unique combination of calming functionalities with nature and physical interactions in a wearable object. We wanted this TUI to help users find relaxation or focus in via soothing sensory demarcation with an aesthetic element while wearing this vest

Initial sketches

  • We imagined a wearable cloth system that relieves stress through sketches. We specifically chose a tangible system because we want users to experience touch with nature and physical interactions as they relax instead of using digital interfaces
  • We imagined a cloth that has LEDs and vibrating motors controlled by a built-in Arduino board. When the user wants to take a break from screens, they can put the cloth on and touch a light-controlled sensor/photocell. The switch will allow users to trigger a pre-programmed array of vibrations and associated LEDs to light up. This vibration-based system is meant to calm the user and provide a sense of relaxation and focus
  • We presented our prototype to the CS 320 class and they gave us feedback that having a customizable set of light patterns and vibrations would be more helpful for users so we changed our initial design and incorporated a slider

Vertical and Horizontal Prototype

  • We explored the form of the vest as part of the horizontal prototype. We chose appropriate cloth material for the layers, took the measurements, and added an embroidered flower to replicate nature as an aesthetic element.
  • For the risky part of the project, we chose to understand how the circuit would work with just one motor and one LED so we configured that. We varied one motor with 4 modes of vibrations ( each vibration has less delay between the waveforms as the slider is moved up). Simultaneously the LED also changes intensity as the slider is varied.

FUNCTIONING PROTOTYPE

The final prototype is composed of the vest made of fabric and the circuitry inside. The vest is arranged as follows:

  • The vest was made using multiple pieces of fabric stitched together. The topmost layer of the vest is composed of 4 individual cloth pieces. For now, they are secured with velcro so that users can take that layer off to examine the circuit in case something stops working
  • The lights are sewn on the external layers along with the slider potentiometer so that users can see the lights (aesthetic feature) as the potentiometer varies and feel the vibrations(which is a feature of stress relief)
  • The first picture shows the circuitry diagram. The circuit is composed of a battery that is connected to the Arduino UNO. The Arduino UNO controls the 4 motors connected in parallel through the haptic driver as well as 4 parallel LEDs that respond to inputs from the slider potentiometer
  • All the circuit components such as the Arduino, the breadboard, and extra wires have been secured by sewing through thread. Also, foam has been placed inside so that users donâ€™t feel the circuit components
Note: The demo was filmed before the top layer was installed since we needed to show the vibrations inside the vest so the components were secured by tape at first but in the final prototype (once the circuit was completely working) the components were secured by sewing (it was hard to show the circuit after the top layer was installed since we did not want to risk moving wires and breaking the circuit)
